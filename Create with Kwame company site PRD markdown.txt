# Product Requirements Document  
# Create With Kwame Website

## Version

**Version 1.0**

## Brand

**Create With Kwame**

## Core Positioning

Create With Kwame helps people **learn AI, build better projects, and implement smarter systems** without getting lost in the technology.

The site should position Kwame as an:

**AI Educator • Builder • Strategist**

The brand should feel premium, practical, human, future-ready, and trustworthy.

The website should not feel like a generic AI consultant site. It should feel like a high-end digital studio and education brand that helps families, schools, professionals, teams, and organizations prepare for an AI-integrated world.

---

# 1. Primary Website Goal

The website must help visitors quickly understand:

1. AI is already part of everyday life.
2. Most people are passively consuming AI without using it intentionally.
3. Kwame helps people use AI correctly, responsibly, and practically.
4. Kwame can help visitors in three clear ways:
   - **Learn**
   - **Build**
   - **Implement**
5. ParentPrep is a flagship product for helping kids build confidence and critical thinking through real-world scenario practice.
6. Visitors should know exactly where to click based on their need.

The homepage should act like a **guided lobby**. It should not over-explain everything. It should help the visitor quickly choose the right path.

---

# 2. Target Audiences

## Primary Audience Groups

### Families and Parents

Parents who want to prepare their kids for the future, help them use AI correctly and responsibly, and build confidence, critical thinking, and real-world decision-making skills.

### Schools and Education Groups

Schools, educators, homeschool groups, PTAs, and education organizations that need practical AI education, responsible AI training, student preparation, and parent engagement support.

### Professionals and Creators

Professionals, founders, entrepreneurs, creators, and business owners who need help turning ideas, drafts, presentations, content, visuals, workflows, or digital products into finished work.

### Teams and Organizations

Small businesses, nonprofits, agencies, schools, departments, and organizations that want to implement AI into real workflows, automations, agents, playbooks, and systems.

---

# 3. Core User Paths

The site must guide users into four paths.

## Path 1: Learn

For people who want AI education, courses, workshops, or training.

## Path 2: Build

For parents who want kids to practice real-world decision-making and critical thinking.

# 8. Animation and Motion Principles

Motion should be smooth, subtle, and meaningful.

Use motion to guide attention, not to show off.

## Required Animation Style

- slow floating hero object
- soft glowing orbit lines
- section fade-up reveals
- staggered card entrances
- hover lift on cards
- CTA button glow on hover
- arrow movement on hover
- slight image zoom on portfolio cards
- sticky nav blur on scroll
- reduced motion support

## Avoid

- fast spinning objects
- flashing lights
- too much parallax
- text flying from multiple directions
- animations that slow mobile
- unnecessary animation on every tiny element

## Recommended Library

Use **Framer Motion** for Version 1.

Optional later:

- GSAP for advanced scroll storytelling
- Spline for interactive 3D
- React Three Fiber for custom 3D

# 22. Copy Rules

## Keep Text Short

Homepage copy should be concise.

Use:

- headings
- one-sentence explanations
- short cards
- labels
- CTAs
- visual examples

## Use Progressive Disclosure

Homepage gives the overview.

Service pages give details.

Contact form captures specifics.

## Avoid Overclaiming

Do not say:

- award-winning
- thousands served
- enterprise-grade
- fully autonomous agents
- guaranteed results
- live product if retired
- app if it is only a workflow

- live product
- retired product
- AI workflow
- project support

# 24. Definition of Done

The site is complete when:

1. Visitors understand the brand within 5 seconds.
2. The homepage clearly explains the problem without too much text.
3. The visitor can choose Learn, Build, Implement, or ParentPrep easily.
4. The design feels premium, spacious, and modern.
5. The site does not look like an overloaded AI-generated website.
6. Each section has a clear purpose.
7. Every card has concise copy.
8. The Learn section emphasizes correct and responsible AI use.
9. The Build section is outcome-first, not AI-first.
10. The Implement section clearly includes workflows, automation, and agents.
11. ParentPrep is clearly presented as the flagship live product.
12. Journeful is clearly labeled as retired.
13. AI workflows are not mislabeled as standalone products.
14. The site works well on desktop, tablet, and mobile.
15. Motion is smooth and not distracting.
16. CTAs route users to the correct next step.
17. The site is fast, accessible, and SEO-ready.
18. The final experience makes visitors think:

**This is the person who can help my family, school, team, or business get ready for an AI-integrated world and solve real problems.**
